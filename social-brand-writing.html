Content Strategy & Campaign Thinking

Travel, but slower.

A campaign strategy sample for a travel brand. The objective was to reposition the brand around slower, more intentional travel — moving away from checklist tourism and toward a more experience-led way of planning trips.

Project

Content-led engagement campaign for a travel / itinerary platform.

Role

Content Writer & Strategist

Objective

Build a campaign concept that shifts the conversation from volume-driven travel to experience-led travel.

Campaign Title
Travel, but slower.
Objective

Reposition travel around depth, not volume.

Reposition the brand around more intentional travel — shifting away from checklist tourism toward slower, experience-led journeys.

Drive:

  • engagement through relatable, insight-led content
  • trust through differentiated positioning
  • conversions through curated, slow-travel itineraries
Audience

Travellers who want more meaning, but less rush.

Urban professionals (25–40) living in high-stimulation environments who:

  • travel 2–3 times a year
  • feel fatigued by rushed, checklist-style trips
  • want more meaningful experiences but don’t know how to plan them differently
  • consume travel content across Instagram, blogs, and community platforms

They are not looking for more destinations.

They are looking for a better way to travel.

Core Insight
People don’t actually enjoy rushed travel — they’ve just never been shown a clear alternative.
Core Message
You don’t need to see more. You need to experience better.
Campaign Idea

Shift the focus from destinations to how people travel.

The campaign shifts the conversation from where to go to how to travel.

Instead of promoting more destinations, it reframes travel as:

fewer places longer stays more local experiences less itinerary fatigue

The campaign doesn’t sell more places.

It sells a better pace.

Campaign Arc

A three-phase journey from attention to action.

Phase 1 — Awareness

Challenge default travel behaviour through contrasts between rushed itineraries and slower, more immersive experiences.

Phase 2 — Consideration

Show what slower travel actually looks like through destination-specific itineraries, neighbourhood guides, and planning content.

Phase 3 — Conversion

Move users toward curated itineraries, trip planners, or guided slow-travel packages through softer, intent-led CTAs.

Goal of the arc

Build emotional connection first, then reduce friction around planning and booking.

Content Pillars

Four ways the idea can take shape.

1. Reframing Travel

Challenge overpacked itineraries, rushed sightseeing, and the pressure to “see everything.”

2. Slower Alternatives

Show what fewer locations, longer stays, and neighbourhood-level travel can actually look like.

3. Practical Planning

Make slow travel actionable through itinerary structure, decision guides, and planning tips.

4. Emotional Payoff

Highlight better memories, less fatigue, and more meaningful travel experiences.

Channels & Formats

Match the message to how people discover and plan travel.

Instagram / Reels

Visual contrasts between rushed and slower travel, local moments, and hooks that interrupt typical travel content.

Blog / SEO Content

Slow-travel itineraries, destination deep-dives, and planning content built around search intent.

Email / Community

Reflections from travellers, curated recommendations, and “trip style” content that keeps the brand useful between bookings.

Purpose

Use each channel differently: attention on social, trust on long-form, and retention through community-driven touchpoints.

Execution Ideas

How the campaign could show up in actual content.

Short-form content

“This is why your trip felt exhausting”

“What slowing down your itinerary changes”

Blog / SEO content

“How to plan a slow travel itinerary”

“1 city, 4 days: a better way to travel”

Visual storytelling

Side-by-side comparisons of rushed vs slower itineraries, and day-in-the-life style travel breakdowns.

Product integration

Curated slow-travel itineraries and neighbourhood-based guides instead of generic city-wide lists.

CTA Direction
Explore a different way to travel Plan your trip with intention
Measurement

Track depth of interest, not just surface engagement.

  • saves and shares on awareness content
  • time spent on blog and itinerary pages
  • click-through rates to curated itineraries
  • conversions or lead capture from planning tools

Iterate based on:

  • content that drives high saves as an intent signal
  • drop-off patterns in long-form or multi-slide content
  • repeat engagement with specific themes or destinations
Why This Works

A campaign built on real travel fatigue.

This campaign aligns with a real shift in how people want to travel — but don’t yet see clearly reflected in most travel content. It offers a genuine alternative, builds emotional connection before conversion, and positions the brand as a thinking partner rather than just a booking platform.
