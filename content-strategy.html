Content Strategy & Campaign Thinking

Travel With Intention

A campaign strategy sample for a travel brand. The objective was to position the brand around slower, more intentional travel — moving away from checklist tourism and toward a more experience-led way of planning trips.

Project

Content-led engagement campaign for a travel/itinerary platform.

Role

Content Writer & Strategist

Objective

Build a campaign concept that shifts the conversation from volume-driven travel to experience-led travel.

Campaign Title
Travel With Intention
Objective

Reposition travel around depth, not volume.

Position the brand as a platform for more intentional travel — moving away from checklist tourism toward slower, experience-led journeys.

Drive:

  • engagement through relatable content
  • trust through thoughtful positioning
  • eventual conversions via curated itineraries
Audience

Travellers who want more meaning, but less chaos.

Young professionals (25–40) who:

  • travel 2–3 times a year
  • are fatigued by “top 10 things to do” content
  • want meaningful experiences but don’t know how to plan them
  • are active across blogs, Instagram, and travel communities
Core Insight

People don’t actually enjoy rushed travel; they just don’t know an alternative way to plan it.

Core Message

You don’t need to see more. You need to experience better.

Campaign Idea

Shift the focus from destinations to how people travel.

Instead of selling destinations, the campaign reframes how to travel.

The focus moves from the number of places visited to the depth of experience.

fewer locations longer stays local experiences less itinerary fatigue
Content Pillars

Four ways the idea can take shape.

1. Reframing Travel

Challenge overpacked itineraries, rushed sightseeing, and the pressure to “complete” a destination.

2. Slower Alternatives

Show what fewer locations, longer stays, and neighbourhood-level travel can actually look like.

3. Practical Planning

Make slow travel actionable through itinerary structure, decision guides, and planning tips.

4. Emotional Payoff

Highlight better memories, less fatigue, and more meaningful travel experiences.

Execution Ideas

How the campaign could play out across content.

Short-form content

"5 reasons why your trips feel exhausting”
“The art of pacing down your itinerary”

Blog / SEO content

“How to plan a slow travel itinerary”
“1 city, 4 days: the modern way to travel”

Visual storytelling

Side-by-side comparisons of rushed vs slower itineraries, and day-in-the-life style travel breakdowns.

Product integration

Curated slow-travel itineraries and neighbourhood-based guides instead of generic city-wide lists.

CTA Direction
Explore modern ways to travel Plan your trip with intention
Why This Works

A campaign built on real travel fatigue.

This idea works because it aligns with a feeling many travellers already have but don’t often see reflected in travel content. It differentiates the brand from generic itinerary-based travel content and positions it as a thinking partner, not just a planning tool.
